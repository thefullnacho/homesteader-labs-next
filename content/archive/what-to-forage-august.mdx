# What to Forage in August: Berries, Wild Fruit, and the Last Easy Greens

August is when the land starts paying out. The spring greens are long gone to seed, the fall nuts aren't down yet, and in between sits the best fruit month of the year: hedgerows sagging with blackberries, elderberry heads turning from green to black, huckleberry slopes doing their thing at elevation. If you only forage one month a year, this is the month.

One thing before the list. In season does not mean safe. Every plant below still gets the full treatment from the [wild berry guide](/archive/wild-berry-guide/): identify the whole plant, leaves and stem and habit, or it stays where it grew. August is generous, and generous months make people sloppy.

## The nationwide list

These are in season somewhere near you this month, coast to coast with regional timing shifts.

### Blackberries

Peak. This is the headline act of August almost everywhere in North America. Aggregate berries made of tiny drupelets on thorny canes, and nothing dangerous copies that structure, which makes them the safest calories in the field. Wait for dull black and an easy pull. If it's shiny and tugs back, give it three more days.

### Elderberries

The clusters that were flowers in June are turning purple-black now through September. Flat-topped sprays on a woody shrub with opposite compound leaves, and you cook them, always, raw elderberries are a stomachache with good PR. August is also when their toxic doppelganger peaks, see the warning section below before you pick a single cluster.

### Huckleberries and late wild blueberries

Lowland blueberries are finishing, but August is the month for huckleberries in the mountain West and the late wild blueberries up north. Same family, same check: the five-point crown on the blossom end. No crown, no deal.

### Wild plums and chokecherries

Thicket fruit season. American wild plums blush red-to-purple in August across the Midwest and Plains, and chokecherries hang in long clusters about the same time. Both are stone fruits: flesh is food, pits are not, cherry-family pits carry cyanogenic compounds, so spit like you mean it and don't crush pits into jam.

### The tough summer greens

Purslane and lamb's quarters are the two worth knowing, both at full sprawl in August, both more nutritious than anything you planted on purpose. Purslane is a succulent mat with paddle leaves and a lemony crunch. Its flat-growing lookalike, spurge, bleeds white sap when snapped. Milky sap, drop it.

### Sumac

Those velvet red cones on staghorn sumac are ripe for sumac-ade right now. Red cones upright, fuzzy, on shrubby edges. The white-berried swamp plant people panic about is a different species that doesn't make red cones, if your berries are red and fuzzy, you're fine.

## Regional callouts

**Northeast.** Beach plums along the coast, blackberries and elderberries inland, sumac everywhere, and hazelnuts starting to drop at the very end of the month. After a good rain, chanterelle flushes in the hardwoods.

**Southeast.** Muscadines. If you're south of Virginia, thick-skinned wild grapes the size of marbles are the August prize, check for tendrils and multiple teardrop seeds like any grape. Pawpaws start dropping in the last week of the month if you're lucky and the raccoons are slow.

**Midwest and Plains.** Wild plum thickets, chokecherries, elderberries along every creek, and lamb's quarters in every disturbed acre. Sand plums in the southern Plains make the best jelly you'll ever fight a thorn bush for.

**West and Pacific Northwest.** Huckleberry season in the mountains, the reason entire towns empty out on August weekends. Salal berries on the coast, and blackberries so invasive that picking them is practically a public service. Desert Southwest: prickly pear fruit ripens magenta from now into September, singe the glochids off before they end up in your tongue.

## The August mixup, one more time

Pokeweed ripens in August, right beside the elderberries, purple-black clusters at the same time in the same hedgerows. This is not a coincidence you get to ignore, it's the single most common serious berry mixup of the month. Elderberry: woody shrub, flat-topped spray. Pokeweed: fleshy herb, long grape-like column, smooth stem in traffic-cone magenta. The neon stem is the alarm. ## A word on August mushrooms

Chanterelles and chicken of the woods are both flushing after summer rains, and both have beginner-friendly reputations they only partly deserve. Mushrooms are a different game with different rules, spore prints, gill structure, a local club that can look over your shoulder. Start with [mushroom foraging 101](/archive/mushroom-foraging-101/), and hold the same line: no positive ID, no dinner.

And if you want a second opinion in the field, [our forager](https://build-small-hackathon-forager-field-notes.hf.space/) runs free in your browser and refuses to guess when it isn't sure. That refusal is the feature. An app that always answers is an app that will eventually answer wrong about the one photo that mattered.
